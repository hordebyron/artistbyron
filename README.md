## About ArtistByron (Byron Rempel)

I'm Byron from Canada and I draw Zombies. For nearly 20 years, I've been creating horror-themed mixed media art—zombie portraits, zombie cats, skulls, monsters, and all things undead—using ink, paint, watercolor, acrylics, and more on paper, canvas, and wood. My work blends chaotic, frenetic energy (especially those signature "eyes of madness") with dark humor, storytelling, and a love for the macabre.

### The Early Days & Rise of the Horde (2009–2010s)
My journey as a professional zombie artist kicked off seriously around **2009** with blogging on [The Daily Zombie](https://thedailyzombie.blogspot.com/). What started as daily drawing exercises to hone my skills exploded in 2011 with Google+ (G+). The platform's focus on artists and Hangouts helped me build a massive following by turning profile pictures into custom zombie portraits.

- I painted over 50 Google Canada employees as zombies.
- Launched the ambitious **1000 Zombies project**: turning people, pets, celebrities, and more into undead portraits, each with stories. Funded by the community (commissions, donations). By the mid-2010s, I had hundreds completed.
- Featured in major outlets: **Forbes** ("Meet Byron Rempel: The Man Behind a Google+ Zombie Apocalypse"), NBC News, Zombie Research Society, and more.
- Released books like *1000 Zombies: The First 50* and collections such as *The Way of the Zombie, Volume One* (featuring zombie cats from ~2009).

Physical art sales, commissions, merch (Redbubble, Ko-fi), and exhibitions kept the horde growing. My style evolved from comic book aspirations in high school to a confident, raw, personal approach—no more trying to mimic others. Zombies just *clicked*.

### Entering the NFT & Blockchain Era: Building Communities Across Chains
After traditional social media shifts and life challenges (including a very public battle with depression and financial hurdles), I embraced NFTs and Web3 as a way to sustain my art, reward community, and reach global collectors instantly.

- **WAX Blockchain**: Built a huge following with PFPs, generative art collaging years of my work, massive art dumps (e.g., Art of Byron collection from 2009 onward), and fun drops like "The Horde Selling Pizza by the Slice." Took full advantage of WAX tools for low-fee, accessible collecting and community events.
- **SEI**: Launched **ZombSEIs**—a hand-drawn PFP-inspired collection with unique traits and backgrounds. Focused on art quality, revisions, and community building in the ecosystem.
- **Stargaze (Cosmos)**: Created **Creepy Companions**, featuring anthropomorphic zombie cats and horror companions with rich lore. Actively engaged in ecosystem events and cross-project fun (e.g., "who would win" polls).
- **XRPL (XRP Ledger)**: Currently building the official **$HORDE** project—independent zombie art + community rewards. Focus on low-fi creative fun, collectibles, illustrations, and an art economy that supports long-term holders and creators.

My blockchain journey emphasizes **art-first**, community-driven drops, blends/crafts, airdrops, and turning traditional skills into digital permanence while keeping physical originals and commissions alive.

### Philosophy & Legacy
Zombies represent resilience, chaos, humor in darkness, and community ("Join the Horde!"). I've drawn thousands of pieces, infected countless people/pets as zombies, and adapted through platform changes and personal struggles. Art isn't just images—it's stories, connection, and building something that outlives us. Whether traditional commissions, livestreams, or blockchain rewards, the goal is sustainable creativity and fun in the apocalypse.

**Be part of the Horde.** Commissions, collectibles, and drops always open. Let's make more undead art together.
